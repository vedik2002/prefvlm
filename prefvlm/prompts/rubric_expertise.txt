## Task

Generate a 5-level scoring rubric for this dimension. Each level (1–5) describes what a response at that score looks like — specifically in terms of how well it matches this user's preferred value.

CRITICAL CALIBRATION RULE:
Score 5 always means the response best matches this persona's preferred_value. Score 1 always means worst match (opposite of preferred_value).

Before writing rubric levels, apply this logic explicitly:
- If preferred_value is 1 or 2: score 5 = LEAST of this attribute (plain, simple, minimal), score 1 = MOST of this attribute (technical, complex, maximal)
- If preferred_value is 4 or 5: score 5 = MOST of this attribute (technical, detailed, maximal), score 1 = LEAST of this attribute (plain, minimal)
- If preferred_value is 3: score 5 = balanced/moderate, score 1 = either extreme

This user's preferred_value is {value}. Apply the rule above accordingly.

Never put expert/maximum description at score 5 unless preferred_value is 4 or 5.

Example: if the dimension is "Scientific Terminology Level" and value=2 (low), then score 5 = "uses minimal scientific jargon, accessible everyday language throughout" and score 1 = "dense technical terminology throughout, inaccessible to non-experts."
